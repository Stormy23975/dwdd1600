Who Were the Signers

Famous signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

John Hancock
President of the Second Continental Congress, known for his large and stylish signature.
Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence and third President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Renowned polymath, diplomat, and one of the Founding Fathers of the United States.
John Adams
Leader in the push for independence, later became the second President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
Political philosopher and one of the leaders of the American Revolution.
Charles Carroll
The only Catholic signer and the last surviving signer of the Declaration.
Roger Sherman
The only person to sign all four great state papers of the U.S., including the Declaration.
Robert Morris
Financier of the Revolution and signer of the Declaration, Articles of Confederation, and Constitution.
Richard Henry Lee
Proposed the motion for independence to the Continental Congress.
George Wythe
Distinguished legal scholar and mentor to Thomas Jefferson.

Each of these individaul played a significant role in the founding of the United States and is remembered for their courage in signing the Declaration, and act considered treasonous by the British Crown.
